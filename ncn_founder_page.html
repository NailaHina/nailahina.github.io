Engr Dr Naila Hina is a former engineering university instructor, international award-winning author of over 100 multilingual books, and a poet from Karachi, Pakistan. Her work bridges science, literature, and humanitarian awareness.

Professional Journey

She holds a B.Eng. in Mechanical Engineering from NED University of Engineering & Technology, Karachi, as well as MBA and CMA degrees, alongside multiple honorary doctorates. Dr Hina has served as Continental Manager of Asia at the World Press Agency, editor, translator, debater, and science journalist.

Literary & Cultural Contributions

Dr Hina has authored a wide array of works including Bahisht e Char Saat, Shadows of the Sun, Cosmic Dreams, Labor of Love, Hayat e Javedan, Fragrance of a Cave Girl, and more. She is the first person to translate Imru al Qays' Arabic poetry into Urdu and pioneered the Gammo style of poetry.

Scientific & Humanitarian Focus

Her work emphasizes critical thinking, scientific awareness, and the promotion of peace, education, and cultural exchange. She envisions a united world through multilingual literature and poetry without borders.

Global Recognition

Dr Hina has received numerous awards and accolades for literature, peace, and humanitarian contributions, and her poems have been published and translated internationally, appearing in newspapers, blogs, and YouTube presentations worldwide.

Engr. Dr. Naila Hina is a visionary poet, multilingual author, and former Engineering University instructor from Karachi, Pakistan. She is internationally recognized as a leading cultural strategist, editor, translator, and humanitarian thinker.

Career & Professional Qualifications

She holds a B.Eng. in Mechanical Engineering from NED University of Engineering & Technology, Karachi, as well as MBA and CMA degrees. Dr. Hina has served as Continental Manager of Asia at the World Press Agency and has been an editor, translator, debater, and science journalist.

Literary Contributions

Dr. Hina has authored over 860 multilingual books including Bahisht e Char Saat, Shadows of the Sun, Cosmic Dreams, Labor of Love, Hayat e Javedan, Fragrance of a Cave Girl, and many more. She is the first person to translate Imru al Qays, the father of Arabic poetry, into Urdu and pioneered the Gammo style of poetry.

Scientific & Humanitarian Focus

Her work emphasizes critical thinking, scientific awareness, peace promotion, and cultural exchange. She envisions a united world through multilingual literature and poetry without borders.

Global Recognition

Dr. Hina has received numerous awards and accolades for literature, humanitarian work, and cultural contributions. Her poems and works are published internationally and translated across multiple languages.

Engr. Dr. Naila Hina is a former Engineering University Instructor, an international award-winning author of over 860 multilingual books, and a visionary poet from Karachi, Pakistan. Her work bridges science, literature, and humanitarian awareness, blending mythic, romantic, political, and metaphysical perspectives across multiple languages including Urdu, Persian, Spanish, and English.

Professional Journey

Dr. Hina holds a B.Eng. in Mechanical Engineering from NED University of Engineering & Technology, Karachi, along with MBA and CMA degrees and multiple honorary doctorates. She has served as Continental Manager of Asia at the World Press Agency, editor, translator, science journalist, and academic debater. Her career spans literature, education, and international strategy.

Literary & Cultural Contributions

She is a multilingual author and poet, pioneering the Gammo style of poetry and translating Imru al Qays' Arabic poetry into Urdu for the first time. Her works include Bahisht e Char Saat, Shadows of the Sun, Cosmic Dreams, Labor of Love, Hayat e Javedan, Fragrance of a Cave Girl, and more. Her writing often explores the intersections of memory, destiny, cosmic symbolism, and human consciousness.

Scientific & Humanitarian Focus

Dr. Hina promotes critical thinking, scientific literacy, cultural exchange, and peace-building through literature and education. She envisions a world united through multilingual expression and cross-cultural understanding.

Global Recognition

Her literary and humanitarian contributions have been recognized internationally. Dr. Hina has been awarded multiple accolades, recommended for the Nobel Prize for Literature (2022, 2023), and her works have been published, translated, and shared globally in newspapers, blogs, and presentations worldwide.
